The cost of NiTiPlant, which we developed with the mechanical and software members of our team, is half the cost of a solar panel that produces electrical energy at the same power. In order to increase renewable energy investments in the world, technologies with lower installation costs are needed and our team presented NiTiPlant as a solution to this problem. In addition, our software team has developed an internet server (Monitir) where the instantaneous energy produced by the installed NiTiPlants and their performance can be observed. In this way, even an individual on the other side of the world can learn about the energy produced in solar energy fields.

During the project development phase, various professors were interviewed and their ideas were also discussed. Various physical calculations of the system have been made and its functionality has been proven. In order to intialize the NiTiPlant project, our team participated in the SolveED Youth Innovation Challenge organized by MIT with NiTiPlant. During this process, various designs of the system were made in the Fusion 360 application, various experiments were carried out with the NiTiNol material used in the system and continued publication research. In addition, the first prototype of the NiTiPlant control server Monitor was made and implemented.
